Why slow yoga?

A usual yoga practice involves going from one Asana to another. The only benefit it gives us is a good cardio workout. Traditionally, Asanas were meant to be held in meditation itself. So, what does actually happens to the body when we hold a posture for 2 minutes?

What is Ananda Yoga?

Ananda Yoga is yoga done with awareness for higher awareness. As a true Raja Yoga approach to Hatha Yoga, Ananda Yoga does not focus on forcing the energy up the spine, but using asana, pranayama and mediation techniques to magnetically draw prana up the spine to the brain --- Safely, gently and harmoniously.

Affirmations

Our words, are just a manifestation of the thoughts which are vibrations of our feelings, which make our words and thoughts a very powerful tool. Every asana (posture) we do has a certain quality. Repeating the affirmation mentally helps us tune into the quality and enhance its effect. On the other hand, it also helps us mind our mind to the present moment and not let it wander as we hold an asana.

Paramhansa Yogananda's Energization Exercises

"The greater the will, the greater the flow of energy." Paramhasna Yogananda's Energization Exercises are his unique contribution to the science of yoga. They are a systematic way to increase the flow of life force, strengthen and heal the body, clear and focus the mind, and prepare you for meditation. Their highest purpose is to help experience the your body as energy which makes it a warm up as well as a pranayama.
